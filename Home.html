Welcome to Bangladesh - The New Tiger of South Asia

In a study by Price WaterHouse and Coopers (PwC), Bangladesh will become the world’s 23rd largest economy by 2050. Goldman Sach also estimates that Bangladesh will be one of the “N11” countries (Bangladesh, Egypt, Indonesia, Iran, South Korea, Mexico, Nigeria, Pakistan, Philippines, Turkey & Vietnam) after BRIC (Brazil, Russia, India & China) that will dominate the future world economy. 

And according to the World Economic Forum, the initial key to Bangladesh’s success in meeting these goals and the needs of the people that will help drive the economy will be electricity. Generating equitable and stable electricity will be important for large industries and households to operate steadily and sustainably.

Food self-sufficiency, massive infrastructure development and political stability will built world confidence that Bangladesh is now different and able to compete with rival countries. Bangladeshi’s per capita income has already surpassed that of India & Pakistan, is likely to rise rapidly and many believe that B​angladesh has great potential to become the new Asian Tiger.

The next steps are for Bangladesh to diversify it’s economy, to build quality infrastructure, introduce new environmental technologies to improve the investment climate and quality of life for her people.

Future of Bangladesh

On November 15, 2020, 15 Countries - all 10 members of the Association of South East Asia Nations (ASEAN) and 5 regional partners signed the Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership (RCEP), arguably the largest free trade agreement in history.

The GDP of this grouping amounts to USD 26.2 trillion and Bangladesh has signaled its intention to join this historic economic partnership as it will transcend into a game changing trade partnership in future.    The 15 countries are the fastest growing economies in the world and includes China, Japan and South Korea. Including Australia and New Zealand, RCEP will connect about 30% of the world's people and output. The benefits for Bangladesh will come in many forms, increased market access, create investor's confidence and also improve efficiency on the supply side. 

  Today, Bangladesh is the 36th largest economy in the world, in less than 10 years from now, will emerge as the 26th largest and the Dhaka Chamber of Commerce (DCCI) in 2010 projected that it will become the 30th largest by the year 2030.  

Strong macroeconomic fundamentals, political stability and demographic dividend has added to her strength and the biggest advantage is 56% of her population are aged between 18 to 40 years. It is the 8th largest workforce in the world and supported by a vibrant and growing GDP of USD 320 billion.    Joining up with such partnerships will only propel her advancements further towards a developing country status and help achieve her short term, mid-term and long-term goals and objectives. This will also allow her to have duty-free access to one of the largest market of 2.2 billion inhabitants, supply side benefits of raw materials to create new industries that will ultimately lead to export diversification. This will also bring opportunities for technology transfer through partnerships, joint-ventures, etc. 

  Additionally, it will be imperative that there shall be reforms made to her many policies to help improve competitiveness and the demand for an efficient infrastructure including transport Eco-system will be a mandatory precondition for Bangladesh to accelerate its growth; and gain from the RCEP partnership. Extensive focus must be paid to improve connectivity - including port infrastructure and logistics efficiency, so as to maximize opportunities arising from RCEP.  

Bangladesh shall strive to be the transportation hub for the region, to be the natural gateway between South Asia and Southeast Asia. We welcome you to participate and join us into the next level of economic prosperity at this critical juncture of its development momentum and help Bangladesh to progress and achieve her vision to become a developed country by 2041.  

Do not miss out on this opportunity rather maximize it to solidify Bangladesh's place in the Asian Century.
